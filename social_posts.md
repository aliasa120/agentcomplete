## Facebook
Punjab Chief Minister Maryam Nawaz Sharif has approved a major flood management initiative that includes clearing all designated flood zones across the province within three months and constructing 17 mini dams at various locations to improve water storage and reduce flood risks.

The decision, announced on March 6, 2026, follows a detailed briefing in which officials informed the meeting that Punjab's five major rivers have 1,990 areas categorized as high-risk flood zones, 1,278 as medium-risk, and 3,169 as low-risk. With rainfall expected to be 28 percent above normal this year, the government is moving urgently to strengthen disaster preparedness.

"The chapter of constructions in the riverbed is being closed forever," CM Nawaz stated, directing authorities to strictly enforce the Supreme Court's ban on construction within river passages and waterways. The government has also decided not to provide financial assistance to those who build illegal structures in water channels that collapse during floods.

As part of the comprehensive plan, the Punjab government will restructure the Provincial Disaster Management Authority (PDMA) by establishing eight new wings, introduce inflatable dam technology, and set up District Emergency Operation Centres and regional disaster response units. Additionally, in-principle approval was granted for a dam project in Chiniot on the Chenab River.

The meeting was also informed that restoration work on 186 roads spanning 563 kilometres, 446 culverts, and one bridge damaged during previous floods has been completed, while desilting work will be carried out on 298 drains and flood channels along with 67 drainage systems.
