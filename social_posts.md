## Facebook
Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif has ordered a nationwide crackdown on petrol hoarding, directing authorities to immediately shut down petrol pumps found involved in creating artificial shortages, revoke their licences, and initiate legal proceedings against them.

The directive was issued during a high-level meeting held in Islamabad on Friday, March 6, 2026, convened to review Pakistan's petroleum reserves amid growing concerns over supply disruptions caused by the widening conflict in the Middle East. "Any petrol pump involved in the reprehensible practice of creating artificial shortages should be immediately shut down, its licence revoked and legal action initiated," the Prime Minister's Office stated [1][2].

Despite the concerns, officials informed the meeting that Pakistan currently holds adequate petroleum stocks to meet national requirements — sufficient for approximately 28 days of consumption. However, panic buying across several cities has led to long queues at fuel stations and rapid depletion of stocks at some outlets.

In response to the crisis, Punjab Chief Minister Maryam Nawaz also ordered a zero-tolerance crackdown in the province, mobilising the Punjab Enforcement and Regulatory Authority against hoarders [3]. Meanwhile, petroleum pump owners have denied allegations of hoarding, blaming supply-side issues for the shortages [4].

The government has also announced a Rs55 per litre increase in petrol and diesel prices — the highest-ever hike — as global crude prices surge closer to $100 per barrel amid the regional tensions.
